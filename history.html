History of Formula 1

Formula 1 (F1) began as the European Championship of Grand Prix Motor Racing in 1950 and quickly grew into a global phenomenon. The sport has witnessed countless memorable moments and breakthroughs in technology.

In 2017, the rights to Formula 1 were purchased by American company Liberty Media for $4.4 billion, marking a new era in the sport's history.

The sport is famous for its fast cars, intense rivalries, and dramatic races, making it one of the most-watched sporting events worldwide.

Interesting Facts

  • Fastest Lap: The fastest lap in a Formula 1 race was set by Michael Schumacher at the 2004 Italian Grand Prix with a time of 1:21.046.
  • Most Wins in a Season: Michael Schumacher holds the record for the most wins in a season with 13 victories in 2004.
  • Youngest World Champion: Sebastian Vettel became the youngest world champion in 2010 at the age of 23 years and 134 days.
  • Longest Race: The 2011 Canadian Grand Prix holds the record for the longest race in F1 history, lasting 4 hours, 4 minutes, and 39 seconds due to rain delays.
